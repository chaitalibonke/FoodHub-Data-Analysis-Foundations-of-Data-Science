# FoodHub Data Analysis — Foundations of Data Science

## Overview
This project performs a comprehensive exploratory data analysis on
FoodHub's food order dataset from New York. The analysis covers
univariate and multivariate exploration of order data to uncover
demand patterns, delivery time performance, customer behavior, and
restaurant performance. Business recommendations are provided based
on data-driven insights to help FoodHub enhance customer experience
and improve operations.

---

## Business Context
FoodHub is a food aggregator company that provides customers access
to multiple restaurants through a single smartphone app. The company
wanted to analyze stored order data to understand restaurant demand,
delivery time performance, and customer preferences in order to make
informed business decisions.

---

## Objective
- Analyze the structure and quality of the FoodHub order dataset
- Perform univariate and multivariate exploratory data analysis
- Identify top performing restaurants and most popular cuisines
- Analyze delivery time performance across weekdays and weekends
- Calculate net revenue generated by the company
- Provide actionable business recommendations based on findings

---

## Dataset

- **Source:** FoodHub internal order records
- **File:** foodhub_order.csv
- **Size:** 1,898 rows x 9 columns

  is proprietary company data

## Methodology

### 1. Data Understanding
- Load and inspect the dataset structure
- Check data types and identify missing values
- Generate statistical summary of all numerical columns

### 2. Data Cleaning
- Check for and handle missing values
- Convert rating column from object to numeric using pd.to_numeric()
- Missing or non-numeric ratings were excluded from average rating
  calculations to ensure accurate restaurant performance metrics

### 3. Univariate Analysis
- Histograms for all numerical variables
- Boxplots for cost, food preparation time, delivery time and rating
- Countplots for cuisine type and day of the week distributions

### 4. Multivariate Analysis
- Pairplot of all numerical variables
- Boxplots of rating, cost, and delivery time by cuisine type
- Weekday vs weekend delivery time comparison

### 5. Business Metric Calculations
- Top 5 restaurants by order volume
- Most popular cuisine on weekends
- Percentage of orders costing more than $20
- Top 3 most frequent customers
- Restaurants eligible for promotional offer
- Net revenue calculation using tiered commission structure
- Percentage of orders taking more than 60 minutes end to end
- Mean delivery time on weekdays vs weekends

## Revenue Model
FoodHub charges restaurants using a tiered commission structure.
Net revenue represents FoodHub's commission share only,
not the total order value.

| Order Cost         | Commission |
|--------------------|------------|
| Greater than $20   | 25%        |
| Between $5 and $20 | 15%        |
| $5 or less         | 0%         |

## Key Findings
- Shake Shack dominates order volume with 219 orders, nearly double
  the second highest restaurant
- American cuisine is the most popular on weekends reflecting
  strong domestic food preferences among customers
- 29.24% of orders cost more than $20 representing the highest
  revenue tier for FoodHub commissions
- 736 out of 1898 orders have no rating which limits the ability
  to assess restaurant quality accurately
- Delivery times are significantly faster on weekends (22.47 min)
  compared to weekdays (28.34 min) suggesting lower traffic or
  higher driver availability on weekends
- Only 10.54% of orders exceed 60 minutes total time indicating
  generally efficient operations
- Four restaurants meet the eligibility criteria for promotional
  offers based on rating count greater than 50 and average
  rating greater than 4.0

---

## Recommendations
- Promote the four eligible restaurants through targeted advertising
  to leverage their strong rating performance
- Offer 20% discount vouchers to the top 3 most frequent customers
  (IDs: 52832, 47440, 83287) to improve retention
- Investigate why weekday delivery times are 26% longer than weekend
  times and optimize driver allocation during weekday peak hours
- Address the high unrated order volume (38.8% of all orders) by
  sending post-delivery rating reminders to customers
- Focus marketing efforts on American and other high-demand cuisines
  during weekends to maximize order volume
- Consider pricing incentives for orders above $20 to increase the
  share of high-commission orders in the revenue mix
- Implement a data quality protocol to standardize rating inputs
  and reduce non-numeric entries in the rating column
